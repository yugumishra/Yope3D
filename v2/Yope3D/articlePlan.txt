YOPE3D DEVLOG SERIES — ARTICLE & VIDEO PLAN (v2)
================================================================
Supersedes the previous plan. Changes are summarized in "WHAT CHANGED"
below; the stale sections of v1 are listed in the TODO section under
"DELETE FROM THE OLD PLAN" so nothing gets re-litigated later.


THE THESIS
----------
The series is about PHYSICS. Graphics, ECS, scripting, tooling and
platform work are supporting systems — they appear only where the physics
arc needs their vocabulary, or as float articles published to keep the
series alive during long engineering phases.

The spine, stated once so every article can be checked against it:

    "I keep replacing the core of my engine with better ideas, and my own
     instrumentation decides whether they earned their place."

That spine is what turns 18 posts into one argument. The back half is the
payoff: authored constraints (I shake the ragdoll) -> a learned policy
driving them (the ragdoll shakes itself) -> a policy that abuses the
solver underneath them.


WHAT CHANGED SINCE v1
---------------------
1. ORDER IS NOW PEDAGOGICAL, NOT PIPELINE. v1 taught broadphase ->
   narrowphase -> islands -> solver (the order the code runs in). v2 opens
   with the derivation of a single contact impulse, then the map, then the
   solver, and only then the manifold/narrowphase deep dive. v1's own
   micro-to-macro video already ran in this reverse order and called it
   "the right pedagogical order" — the articles now agree with the video.

2. THE PYRAMID TWIST. The narrowphase article is no longer "SAT +
   manifolds." It is the pyramid-collapse investigation: I was certain it
   was the 20-year-old solver, and it was actually face clipping plus
   warm-start keys derived from a depth-sorted array index. This lands
   MUCH harder placed immediately AFTER the PGS article, because the
   reader has just been handed a solver and told about its convergence
   weaknesses — so they will wrongly blame it too. Teaching through the
   bug.

3. SPRINGS + JOINTS MERGED into one "Constraints" article. Springs, point/
   hinge/cone-twist joints, ragdolls and vehicles are one conceptual
   family solved in one loop. The rope/cloth/soft-body footage becomes the
   opening act; the ragdoll and vehicle are the payoff.

4. THE FRONTIER ARC IS SCHEDULED, NOT DEFERRED. GJK/EPA (v1's "D"),
   plus three new installments: AVBD, ragdoll RL, and the packing agent.

5. CATCH-UP IS NO LONGER A GATE. v1 implied ~14 backlog articles before
   any new work could be published. That is most of a year of writing, and
   the backlog is the LEAST differentiated content in the series. v2 keeps
   only the articles that are load-bearing prerequisites for something
   downstream, moves the rest to FLOAT, and starts frontier publishing as
   soon as each installment's vocabulary prerequisites are met.

6. ONE ECS ARTICLE, not "one or two."

7. THE STRESS-TEST REWORK IS DONE. See DELETE FROM THE OLD PLAN.


DECISIONS CARRIED FORWARD FROM v1 (still valid)
-----------------------------------------------
- Vulkan fundamentals get ONE condensed article, and it is a float, not a
  gate. Graphics is a supporting necessity, not the subject.
- Every article opens with a 2-3 paragraph recap and backlinks; Article 1
  is the master recap/bridge for readers arriving from the Java series.
- Videos are visual-first, single-payoff. Each article gets a paired
  video; extra/unpaired videos are allowed.
- Data-heavy material (profiler tables, analyzer output) stays in the
  article body. Videos overlay 1-2 analyzer plots as stills over footage;
  no pandas screencasts.
- Link out rather than re-derive: Gaffer on Games "Fix Your Timestep,"
  Catto's GDC material, Ericson's RTCD.

  have no live narrowphase branch yet. That is the last line of 12 and the
  first line of 13.

  15 closes it. Say so explicitly in 8; readers reward a promise kept
  seven articles later.

- 13/14 land several articles after the narrowphase piece they extend.

  "remember the manifolds from the pyramid piece? I'm replacing how they
  are generated" is an easy reconnection.

- If a frontier phase stalls, everything before it has already shipped and
  the float list covers the gap. No installment is load-bearing for the
  series' coherence except 4-7, which are all already-built work.

CODE SAMPLE POLICY
================================================================
Article order is now independent of commit order, so the question "which
version of the code do I show?" needs a standing answer. It is NOT
git revert. Three facts settle this:

  1. The pre-ECS solver (859a04e, f0e8720) is built on the physics::Hull
     class hierarchy and ColliderCCD, both DELETED at 197e1db. Checking
     out that tree to write Article 6 would teach an architecture that no
     longer exists in the engine and that I explicitly killed. A reader who
     then opens the repo finds no trace of it.

  2. Those snapshots are ones I labeled broken at the time:
     197e1db = "removed hull (bugs present)",
     e6809fc = "a few fixes (instability still present)".

  3. Git is not an article index and never will be. The manifold fix — the
     entire subject of Article 7 — landed in 9895264, whose commit message
     is "trigger volumes." The commits are milestone-shaped and bundle
     unrelated work. There is no commit whose diff is "the pyramid fix."

THE POLICY:

  A. ARTICLE CODE IS A TEACHING ARTIFACT, NOT A REPO SNAPSHOT. Show the
     CURRENT code, elided, with honest markers. In Article 6, where
     solveIsland's joint block would confuse the contact story:

         // ... joints are solved in this same loop, interleaved with
         // contacts. That's Article 12 — ignore them for now.

     That comment is the best thing in the excerpt: it is a forward-link,
     it is honest about what is hidden, and it tells the reader the joints
     BELONG there. Article 12 opens by un-eliding exactly that block.
     Progressive reveal across the series is a feature, not a compromise.

  B. PIN, DON'T REVERT.
     - Permalink every excerpt at a COMMIT SHA (GitHub: press `y`), never
       at main. Those links never rot.
     - Tag HEAD at publish time: article/06-pgs, article/07-pyramid, etc.
       Tags are just refs — free to create, nothing to maintain — and they
       give readers `git checkout article/06-pgs` for a coherent, compiling
       tree exactly as the article describes it. This is what reverting was
       trying to buy, without touching history.

  C. WHERE THE ARTICLE IS A BEFORE/AFTER (7, 15), DO NOT DO ARCHAEOLOGY —
     KEEP BOTH VERSIONS LIVE IN THE TREE BEHIND A TOGGLE. A runtime toggle
     is strictly better than a git checkout, because it produces the code
     excerpt AND the side-by-side video from the same artifact, reproducible
     by anyone who clones the repo. This is why the toggles below are the
     highest-priority items in the whole work order. AVBD is the extreme
     case (both solvers permanently in the tree behind a flag) and it
     retroactively justifies the pattern for the earlier articles.

  D. For Article 7's "before" manifold code specifically: the fix is buried
     inside the "trigger volumes" commit, so extracting it means hand-
     picking the routine out of `git show 9895264 -- src/physics/
     ColliderAnalytical.cpp` anyway. Once you are hand-extracting, stop
     pretending it is archaeology: clean it up and present it as "the code
     I had, simplified." Nobody is diffing the article against a commit.
     What matters is that the failure it produces is real — and it is.

STRETCH / FUTURE INSTALLMENTS (dependency order, honest estimates)
================================================================
Each is a standalone article. None is on the critical path of the series.

  S1  Joints on AVBD                      ~1.5-2 wk   (needs #15)
      C / Jacobian / bounds per type — the PGS "menu of constraints"
      recipe carries over almost one-to-one. Position-level, so no drift
      and no per-constraint stabilization tuning.

  S2  Ragdoll Rematch on AVBD             ~1 wk       (needs S1, #16)
      Retrain the policy against the new solver; compare exploits. Mostly
      compute time.

  S3  Springs Migrated Into the Solve     ~0.5 wk     (needs S1)
      Stiff springs stop being a stability problem — unconditionally
      stable at any stiffness. The flag-on-pole / stiffness-ratio demos.

  S4  Restitution for a Position Solver   ~2-3 days   (needs #15)
      AVBD is inelastic by construction (implicit Euler + position-level
      contacts). Bolt on a velocity-level pass. Unlocks bouncy A/B scenes.

  S5  AVBD on the GPU (avbd.comp)         ~3-4 wk     (needs #15)
      One invocation per body per color pass. The 100k-body-pile footage
      nothing else in this engine class can match. THE SERIES FINALE, and
      the single most deferrable thing on the roadmap — do not let it
      creep into #15.

  S6  The Packing Agent, Phase 2:         ~2+ wk      (needs #18)
      irregular containers (compound distance loop + a voxel containment
      mask) and compound ITEMS (nested set-pooling). Honest research
      territory; scope carefully.

  S7  AI Learns Architecture              ~5.5-6.5 wk (needs nothing new)
      Evolutionary floor-plan generation scored by an embodied navigator,
      compared against real buildings imported via glTF. Exercises the
      static-compound/BVH pipeline. Reuses NOTHING from the frontier arc —
      which is both its virtue (no coupling risk) and its weakness (no
      reinforcement of the roadmap). A separate series, not an installment.
